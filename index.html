Pacing strategy by mile
Mi 1–2 (Verrazzano): climb then a long fast descent into Brooklyn. Run the climb by effort; let the descent come to you.

Mi 2–13 (Brooklyn): flattest, most stable stretch. Settle into goal MP — bank effort, not time.

Mi 15 (Queensboro): ~3/4mi quiet, unshaded uphill right as early fatigue sets in, no crowd noise. Expect to lose a few sec/mile — don't force it back immediately.

Mi 16 (First Ave): the crowd noise is a wall of sound and pace often creeps up here without you noticing — hold your number.

Mi 16–19: subtle, gradual false-flat climb through Manhattan/Bronx. Stay controlled — this is where banked time from Brooklyn gets spent if you're not careful.

Mi 20–21 (the Bronx): classic late-race fade territory. Fueling discipline from the first half pays off here.

Mi 22–23 (Fifth Ave): a long, gradual ~2% climb that feels steeper this late — a slight slowdown here is normal.

Mi 24–26.2 (Central Park): rolling short hills to the finish. Save something for these in training long runs, not just on race day.

Fueling: gels for any effort over 1:30, starting ~mile 4–5, repeating every 30–45 min. Rehearse the exact schedule on the Week 13 20-miler, not for the first time on race day.
